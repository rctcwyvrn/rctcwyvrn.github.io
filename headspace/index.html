A description of a plural person's headspace

Posted on 2026-04-23 by [skye, lotus]

Hi, Skye here!

You know Skyblock in Minecraft? Our headspace is a skyblock-esque set of floating islands.

Here's a short description of the rooms and how we built them.

The Fronting Room

An undecorated room with white walls and a study wooden table. At the table are wooden chairs with each headmate's name engraved upon it.

At one head of the table sits Lily, on the other end sits Cedar. The others are sat around the rest of the lengths of the table.

The seats represent fronting/co-fronting status, when one sits in their chair they are part of the front.

This room was the first room we constructed in our headspace, and thus is the most simple and straightforward in it's construction and purpose.

It's meant to give a visual representation of the system and who's fronting. It's just chairs and a table.

The Quiet Space

A quiet space for the children to rest.

All of the children yearn for gentle silence so that's what this room is. Fabric lined walls to absorb sound and bookshelves everywhere else. Soft seating and carpet.

The Meadow

A grassy meadow with a tree for us to sunbathe and rest in nature. We needed a space of nature in our headspace so we built this!

The Rain Room

A perpetually raining room with a small river. A melancholic blue room.

Plays the Hollow Knight City of Tears OST.

Skye's dance room

A room for me! A dance hall with mirrors and ribbons and everything that I need to dance and enjoy myself.

The construction process

We built out headspace iteratively, one by one we asked the members and found what kinds of rooms we wanted and built them out as needed.

I'm sure we'll build out more islands as time passes, but this is what we've got right now.

I guess I'm the one with the most insight into building out headspace? Creative director I guess.

I like being able to sketch out this space and build something that's comfortable for everyone.
